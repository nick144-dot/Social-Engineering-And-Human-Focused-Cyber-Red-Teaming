# Social-Engineering-And-Human-Focused-Cyber-Red-Teaming
Exploring how attackers exploit human behavior in cyber environments through social engineering, real-world scenarios, and red teaming techniques.
## 🌐 Communities

This is where I’d actually go to learn, ask questions, and see how people think through real problems. If you're just getting into cybersecurity or red teaming, I’d start with structured platforms like TryHackMe, then move into Hack The Box, and use communities like Reddit and Discord to fill in gaps, ask questions, and stay current.

---



### 📰 Vice Tech / Motherboard
🔗 https://www.vice.com/en/section/tech/  

Vice’s technology and cybersecurity section (formerly known as Motherboard), covering topics like hacking, privacy, and digital security. Good for staying current on real-world tech issues and how cybersecurity impacts society. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

🔗 https://haveibeenpwned.com/  

A tool that lets you check if your email or accounts have been exposed in known data breaches. Useful for understanding how often personal data is leaked and improving your own security practices.


### 🧠 Hack The Box (Platform + Discord)
🔗 https://www.hackthebox.com/  
🔗 https://discord.gg/hackthebox  

One of the best places to actually *do* cybersecurity, not just read about it. The labs are hands-on and force you to think like an attacker, and the Discord is very active with people at all skill levels. Good mix of learning, challenges, and networking.

## 📚 Writeups / Case Studies

These are examples of real-world attacks or interesting security concepts that show how vulnerabilities are actually exploited. I tried to include a mix of technical and human-focused scenarios.

---

### 🕵️ FBI Cyber Most Wanted
🔗 https://www.fbi.gov/wanted/cyber  

Collection of wanted cyber criminals involved in things like ransomware, fraud, and nation-state attacks. This is useful for seeing real-world adversaries and how cyber attacks actually happen outside of lab environments.

---

### 🐕 Detection Dogs for Electronic Devices
🔗 https://www.wired.com/story/digital-detection-dogs/  

Some law enforcement agencies are now training dogs to detect electronic devices like USB drives, hard drives, and phones by smelling chemical components used in storage devices. This is interesting because it shows how even physical concealment of data can be defeated using non-traditional methods.

---

### 🎣 Phishing Attacks (General Case Studies)
🔗 https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/news/avoiding-social-engineering-and-phishing-attacks  

Phishing is still one of the most effective attack methods because it targets people, not systems. These types of attacks show how small mistakes (like clicking a link) can lead to major breaches.

---

### 🏢 Target Data Breach (2013)
🔗 https://www.csoonline.com/article/566196/the-target-hack-how-they-did-it.html  

Attackers gained access through a third-party HVAC vendor, not directly through Target’s systems. This is a good example of how attackers look for the weakest link, not the most obvious one.

## 🎤 Events / Conferences

These are some of the main conferences where new tools, techniques, and research are shared. Even if you don’t attend in person, a lot of them publish talks and content online.

---

### 🕶️ DEF CON
🔗 https://defcon.org/  

One of the largest and most well-known hacking conferences. Covers everything from red teaming and social engineering to hardware hacking and AI. A lot of cutting-edge research and tools are first shown here.

---

### 🔓 Security BSides
🔗 http://www.securitybsides.com/  

More community-driven and accessible than larger conferences. BSides events happen all over the world and focus on practical, real-world security topics without the corporate feel.

---

### 🧠 Black Hat
🔗 https://www.blackhat.com/  

More formal and training-focused than DEF CON, with in-depth briefings and technical sessions. A lot of high-level research and vulnerabilities are presented here.

### 🧠 Social Engineering in Real Attacks (Darknet Diaries Example)
🔗 https://darknetdiaries.com/episode/94/  

Real-world story showing how attackers manipulate people to gain access rather than relying purely on technical exploits. These kinds of cases highlight how important human behavior is in cybersecurity.
